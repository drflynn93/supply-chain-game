Sports Marketing Edition
A long time ago, in a supply chain far, far away...
The IRAN WAR has thrown galactic trade into chaos. The Strait of Hormuz — the great artery of the known universe — stands CLOSED. Qatari and UAE energy exports have gone dark. The shipping giants Maersk and Hapag-Lloyd have fled the Middle Eastern theater entirely.
Ports around the Indian Ocean overflow with stranded cargo. A shipment of hair clips and playdough drifts helplessly off Karachi, costing its desperate owner $200 a day in fees. More than 100 vessels are trapped in the Gulf. The Empire of Global Trade trembles.
Meanwhile, in the warehouse sector, a rebellion stirs. Big-box vacancies hit multi-year highs. Cold-storage rates collapse. The Rebel Alliance of Third-Party Logistics scrambles — DHL marshals 10 new outposts, and FedEx raises its market-cap flag over a battered UPS.
And then — from out of the chaos — a beacon of hope. With 1.3 seconds remaining, UConn guard Braylon Mullins receives the inbound pass, takes one dribble, and launches a 35-foot buzzer-beater over a helpless Duke defender. Huskies 71, Blue Devils 70. The galaxy erupts.
Within seconds, 200,000 fans flood team stores demanding Mullins jerseys. Licensed apparel supply chains around the known universe strain under the surge. Someone must get those $50 jerseys to the fans — fast, and at a profit.
That someone is YOU. Armed with knowledge of cost tradeoffs, supplier risk, and inventory strategy, you must serve the fans before the moment fades.
Revenue. Cost. Profit. Only through mastery of all three can order be restored to the supply chain.
