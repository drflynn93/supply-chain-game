EPISODE XII

THE STRAIT
STRIKES BACK
The IRAN WAR has thrown galactic trade into chaos. The Strait of Hormuz — the great artery of the known universe — stands CLOSED. Qatari and UAE energy exports have gone dark. The shipping giants Maersk and Hapag-Lloyd have fled the Middle Eastern theater entirely.
Ports around the Indian Ocean overflow with stranded cargo. A shipment of hair clips and playdough drifts helplessly off Karachi, costing its desperate owner $200 a day in demurrage fees. More than 100 vessels are trapped in the Gulf. The Empire of Global Trade trembles.
Meanwhile, in the warehouse sector, a rebellion stirs. Big-box vacancies have hit multi-year highs. Cold-storage rates have collapsed. The Rebel Alliance of Third-Party Logistics scrambles to fill the void — DHL marshals 10 new outposts across North America, and FedEx has raised its market-cap flag over the ruins of a battered UPS.
In this hour of crisis, ONE supply chain manager must rise. Armed only with a balanced scorecard and hard-won knowledge of cost tradeoffs, supplier risk, and inventory strategy, they must serve the fans — delivering jerseys, equipment, and licensed apparel to a fractured galaxy of consumers.
The Balanced Scorecard awaits. COST. SPEED. FILL RATE. Only through mastery of all three can order be restored to the supply chain.
